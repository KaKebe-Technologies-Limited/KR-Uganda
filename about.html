About Us

Our Background

Kids at Risk Uganda is a non-governmental, non-profit organisation (NGO) registered to operate countrywide based in Lira City, Northern Region, East Africa. Journalist Frank Okot established the NGO in 2018 to support vulnerable children and youths, and protects their interests and rights. The organisation has staff, board, and volunteers.

In Uganda, the organisation currently operates in Lira district, Lango Sub-region, Northern Uganda and Kiryandongo Refugee Settlement, a refugee camp based in Bweyale Town Council, Kiryandongo district, Bunyoro Sub-region, Western Uganda, now a home for more than 100,000 refugees, including their children from the Democratic Republic of Congo, Kenya, Rwanda, Burundi, and South Sudan who fled their countries as a result of the civil war to seek refuge into the country. The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) manages the settlement and they provide shelter, land, and support for them [refugees].


Our Vision

To let children and youths have equal rights, opportunities and access to quality services.

Our Goal

To improve the welfare of vulnerable children and youths, regardless of discrimination, faith, gender and background.

Our Values

We don’t just focus on what we do – we also care how we do it. We’ve 13 values everyone at Kids at Risk Uganda shares. They’re what we expect from each other and ourselves. These values are not just words. We use them to guide our day-to-day decisions/operations and the way we behave when we’re working with vulnerable groups and other people. Here’re the 13 values: Positive, honest, integrity, respect, responsibility, love, protection and care, confidentiality, gender equity, teamwork, transparency and helping the needy.

Our Projects Focus Areas

Sustainable Livelihoods, Support Services, Global Health and Community Development, Education and Sports, Community Outreach, Climate Change Mitigation, Environment Conversation, Promotion of Peace and Reconciliation, Human Rights and Access to Justice, Good Governance, Research, Improved Access to Water, Sanitation and Hygiene Practices, and Provision of Spiritual Support.

Our Achievements

Supported 578 vulnerable people, including 260 children with food and non-food items such as sanitary pads (for girls), pens, pencils, exercise books, textbooks, mathematical sets, Christmas gifts, laundry soap, rice, beans, posho, salt, eggs, cooking oil, sugar, tealeaves and clothes among other items worth Shs5.9m to the prison, hospitals and babies’ homes.

Trained a total of 1,251 people in environmental conservation, water and child protection, human rights, advocacy for reproductive health and sexual gender-based violence, vocational skills, financial literacy, leadership and entrepreneurship development, mentorships, life-skills, and other economic and social empowerment activities.

Carryout out education and counseling programme in schools and communities, Community campaign on reproductive health and sexual gender-based violence, Advocacy to support Youths to equal access to property, markets and productive employment/livelihoods and Campaign on youth empowerments and adolescent.

Conducted community dialogues and stakeholders meetings, Bi annual review meeting with youth groups, community facilitators and community leaders and quarterly joint monitoring, learning and evaluation with district, sub-county, donor representatives and other stakeholders

Sensitised the masses on HIV/AIDS, Malaria, and TB among other diseases within the 10 tropics through different platform such as posters, illustrations, radio talk shows, media advocacy and compelling dramas about the kids and the youth on their vulnerability.

Distributed Covid-19 supplies such as disposable surgical facemasks, hand wash sanitizers to communities to stop the spread of the virus.

Reunited 20 street teens with their family members.

Rehabilitated 10 broken boreholes
